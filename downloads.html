Sample statements

You will find below four statements from our Archives, to showcase the sort of encounters our Institute records and researches.

Please note that, though these four people have graciously allowed us to showcase their statements on our website, our statements are not usually accessible to the general public, as we aim to ensure the anonymity of our statement givers.
If you are a researcher or other member of an academic institution and you would like to access one of our statements, please contact us at mail@themagnusinstitute.org.

1. Regarding a spectral figure in Gunnersbury Triangle, London. Download statement...
In retrospect I figure I must have fallen asleep, but that’s not what it felt like at all. There was no slow drifting away, there was no drowsy awakening, all I know is that it was bright daylight and then it was pitch black.

2. Regarding frequent sensations of falling. Download statement...
I felt a slight shift, something that should have been impossible given that the shelf was supposedly attached to the wall, and then a nearly imperceptible breeze. Suddenly my body slipped and I was falling, like being pushed over a balcony I hadn't known was there.

3. Regarding a series of zombie apocalypse nightmares. Download statement...
The night before my first day on placement, I was so excited I could barely sleep, but for the first time since I’d started my degree, I also felt nervous. I didn’t connect it to everything else until much later, but my dreams that night were vivid and strange, and I spent hours walking around a deserted, scorching Cambridge where everything felt familiar but wrong.

4. Regarding the role of blankets in a mysteriously foggy encounter. Download statement...
I am here because, well, the blanket did something. My blanket did something, anyway. Or, maybe it wasn’t actually the blanket, but something else. It helps me feel better if I ascribe blame to the blanket, though, because if it wasn’t the blanket, I think I’m in big trouble.
